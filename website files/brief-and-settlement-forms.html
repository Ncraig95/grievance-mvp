Brief and Settlement Forms

These forms collect detailed grievance information for union review, case preparation, and settlement documentation. They should be filled out carefully because the information becomes part of the grievance record.

Important: If any form will not open, will not submit, loses information, or produces a document that looks wrong, contact the hall immediately.

True Intent Grievance Brief

Use this form when: A grievance needs a True Intent brief prepared for review or escalation.

How to fill it out: Enter the existing grievance number, grievant contact information, job information, local information, occurrence date, grievance type, issue, articles, management structure, step dates, timeline, argument, analysis, company position, union position, strengths, weaknesses, proposed settlements, and attachment names.

What it should do: The system generates the True Intent brief and routes it into the grievance workflow.

Non-Discipline Grievance Brief

Use this form when: The grievance is not discipline related and a non-discipline brief is needed.

How to fill it out: Enter the grievance number, grievant name, local number, location, work group, home address, dates, issue or condition, action taken, facts, analysis, current status, union position, company position, witnesses, recommendation, and attachments.

What it should do: The system generates the Non-Discipline Grievance Brief and files it through the intake workflow.

Disciplinary Grievance Brief

Use this form when: The grievance involves discipline and a disciplinary brief is needed.

How to fill it out: Enter the grievance number, grievant contact information, job information, local information, occurrence date, discipline date, grievance type, articles, step dates, current status, disparate treatment, related grievances, outside remedies, company facts and argument, union facts and argument, representation information, proposed settlements, and attachment names and dates.

What it should do: The system generates the Disciplinary Grievance Brief and routes it into the grievance workflow.

Settlement Form 3106

Use this form when: A settlement needs to be documented for an existing grievance.

How to fill it out: Enter the grievance number, manager signer email, steward signer email, grievant name, grievant email, informal meeting date if applicable, company representative, union representative, issue article, issue details, and settlement terms.

What it should do: The system generates Settlement Form 3106 and routes it for electronic signatures from the company representative or manager and the steward.
